From Adam, Tessa's husband:

It is devastating when the doctor says to your beautiful and vibrant wife, "I am sorry but your cancer is back." The world spins a little more slowly and time seems much more precious. We heard these words in Oct. of 2010 and thought we were past it until they were said again in Feb. of 2012.

This will be Tessa's 3rd battle with this awful disease. In 1998, at the age of 16, Tessa battled Hodgkin's Disease and went into remission. Twelve years later, the cancer came back, and it was a little further advanced and more aggressive than the first time, stage IVB. After extensive chemotherapy with ICE and an Autologous Stem Cell Transplant Tessa was in remission... Until her six month PET/CT scan failed. Now, a year after the transplant Tessa is battling cancer a 3rd time. She faces a cutting-edge treatment (Adcetris) and an Allogenic Bone Marrow Transplant (hopefully from one of her 3 brothers).

Tessa has so many qualities that made me fall in love with her back in 2004. Her smile captivated me, it still lights up any room. Her strength inspired me, now it empowers me. I love her tenacity in facing difficulty. I love how beautiful she is every day, even when she does not feel it. The scars on her body only endear me more to her because it is part of OUR history. I love that Tessa is a great mother to our two children, Mykelle (4) and Evan (3). I love that they adore Tessa and always want to impress her. I love that Tessa teaches them so many good habits. I will always love Tessa for all of the good she has put into the world. How can I not say that I love her more now than I did when we first met?

Tessa's fight with cancer has opened my eyes to the world of kindness and compassion. I did not realize how much fighting cancer depended upon community. Family and friends watched our children during numerous hospital visits. People brought us food and gave us recipes for cancer fighting diets. Prayers and affection were expressed through so many letters, phone calls, emails, and Facebook posts. Family and friends made the holidays special while Tessa was getting chemo over Christmas. The many good deeds illuminated some of the darkest moments.

Getting Tessa healthy and maintaining a semblance of normalcy for our children become priorities. Sadly, overcoming cancer is not the only hardship our family faces during a long treatment process. It feels wrong to worry about money when your wife is battling for her life but it is a reality. Despite having insurance coverage, the financial strain of fighting cancer weighs heavily on us. Between taking a lot of time of work, frequent hospital visits, medication and medical bills, the treatment cost is high. Tessa's treatment is worth every struggle we might encounter. She is a fighter and will beat this a 3rd time.

Thank you for taking time out of your busy lives to watch Tessa's video and support her in whatever way that may be. The kindness you offer will resonate in our lives more than you may ever know.


Tessa Winger is a 2 time cancer survivor and has been recently diagnosed with Hodgkin's Lymphoma for the 3rd time. She is 30 years old, married, with two young children. We, her family and friends, are trying to do everything we can to help them through this difficult time. Any help you can offer this amazing family as they fight the cancer battle yet again would be greatly appreciated.
